Research

My research combines optical photometry, radio spectroscopy, and numerical modeling to study exoplanets, open clusters, and Galactic neutral hydrogen.

Exoplanets in Open Clusters

For my M.S. thesis at IST Islamabad, I carried out a photometric search for short-period, Jupiter-size planets in the open clusters NGC 884 and NGC 869 using a 0.8 m telescope. Although no hot Jupiters were detected, the survey yielded 25 variable stars (including four new candidates) and placed upper limits on the occurrence rate of cluster hot Jupiters.

The work highlights the role of young, coeval stellar populations as laboratories for testing planet-formation and migration scenarios in dense environments.

Transit Timing Variations

As a collaborator with ÇOMÜ Ulupınar Observatory, I work on transit timing variation (TTV) analyses of short-period planetary systems. By combining high-precision ground-based transit photometry with MCMC transit fitting and N-body integrations, we search for dynamical signatures of non-transiting companions.

This work targets systems where small deviations from a linear ephemeris can reveal hidden planets, constrain masses, and probe orbital resonances.

Galactic H I 21 cm Emission

In a dedicated project on neutral hydrogen, I used the 1.5 m PICTOR radio telescope to obtain 600 s drift-scan spectra of Galactic H I 21 cm emission. My pipeline includes RFI mitigation, baseline subtraction, Gaussian profile fitting, and conversion to local standard of rest (LSR) velocities.

Recovered column densities agree with the LAB survey at the ~15% level, demonstrating that small-aperture telescopes can provide meaningful constraints on Galactic structure and interstellar gas dynamics in a student-accessible setting.

Space Architecture & Self-Healing Materials

Within the Deep Space Initiative’s Space Architecture program, I contribute to the concept of a Neuro-Responsive Regenerative Window System (NRWS) for modular space habitats. My role includes thermo-mechanical modeling of adaptive, self-healing materials under orbital thermal cycles and mechanical loads, using tools such as COMSOL and ANSYS.

This project connects my astrophysical background with space systems engineering and long-duration human spaceflight challenges.
