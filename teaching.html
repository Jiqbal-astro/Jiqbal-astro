Teaching

I have taught physics and mathematics at the college level and enjoy designing inquiry-based activities that connect classroom topics to modern astrophysics and space science.

College-level Physics Lecturing

As a Physics Lecturer at Government and STEM Colleges in Pakistan (2023–2024), I taught undergraduate-level courses in mechanics, electromagnetism, waves, and modern physics. My teaching emphasized conceptual understanding, problem-solving skills, and the connection between classical physics and contemporary astrophysics.

Student Projects & Mentoring

I have supervised and advised student projects on topics such as exoplanet detection, variable stars, and basic radio astronomy. I encourage students to work with real data whenever possible, using open-source tools (Python, astropy, lightkurve) and public survey archives.

Teaching Goals

Looking ahead, I hope to integrate more open-source simulations, remote observing opportunities, and data-driven labs into astronomy and physics courses. My long-term goal is to develop curricula that make exoplanet and radio astronomy accessible to students in resource-limited settings.
